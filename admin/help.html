Getting Started

Welcome to the CMS

The Luxury Travel Sweden Content Management System (CMS) allows you to manage all content on your website without needing to write code. This documentation will guide you through each section of the CMS.

Important: All changes you make in the CMS go live on the website immediately when you click "Save" or "Publish". There is no staging environment, so always double-check your content before saving.

Common Features

Most CMS pages share these common features:

  • Status Toggle: Switch between Draft and Published to control visibility on the website
  • Rich Text Editor: Format text with bold, italic, headings, and more
  • Image Upload: Upload images directly or select from the Media Library
  • Search & Filter: Find content quickly using the search bar
  • Drag & Drop Ordering: Reorder items by dragging them up or down

Publishing Workflow

1Create or edit content

2Save as Draft to preview without publishing

3Review content on the live site (use "View Site" button)

4Set status to Published when ready to go live

Dashboard

Overview

The Dashboard is your home page in the CMS. It provides a quick overview of your website's content and recent activity.

What You'll See

  • Content Statistics: Total counts of destinations, blog posts, stories, and team members
  • Recent Leads: Latest contact form submissions from your website
  • Quick Actions: Shortcuts to create new content
  • Recent Activity: Latest updates across all content types
Tip: Use the Dashboard to get a quick pulse on your website activity. Check it regularly to see new lead submissions.

Destinations

What Are Destinations?

Destinations are the locations you offer to your clients. Each destination appears on your website with rich details including descriptions, images, activities, and pricing information.

How to Add a New Destination

1Click the "Add New Destination" button

2Fill in the required fields:

  • Title: Name of the destination (e.g., "Swedish Lapland")
  • Slug: URL-friendly version (e.g., "swedish-lapland") - auto-generated from title
  • Description: Short overview that appears in destination cards
  • Hero Image: Main image shown at the top of the destination page
  • Gallery Images: Additional images shown in the photo gallery

3Add detailed content:

  • Long Description: Full destination details with rich formatting
  • Activities: Things visitors can do at this destination
  • Best Time to Visit: Seasonal recommendations
  • Duration: Typical trip length (e.g., "5-7 days")

4Set pricing and availability

5Save as Draft or set to Published

Managing Destination Images

  • Use high-quality images (minimum 1920px wide for hero images)
  • Landscape orientation works best for hero images
  • Upload multiple gallery images to showcase the destination
  • Add descriptive alt text for accessibility
Important: Published destinations appear immediately on the website. Use Draft status while preparing content.

Leads & Contacts

What Are Leads?

Leads are contact form submissions from your website. When visitors fill out the contact form, their information appears here.

Managing Leads

  • View Details: Click any lead to see full message and contact information
  • Filter by Status: Toggle between New, Contacted, and Converted
  • Update Status: Mark leads as you progress through your sales process
  • Search: Find leads by name, email, or message content
  • Delete: Remove spam or irrelevant submissions

Lead Workflow

1New leads arrive with "New" status

2Review the inquiry and contact the potential client

3Update status to "Contacted" after initial outreach

4Change to "Converted" when they become a client

Tip: Check the Leads page daily to ensure prompt responses to inquiries. Quick response times improve conversion rates.

Exporting Leads

Use the "Export to CSV" button to download all leads for use in your email marketing or CRM system.

LIV AI Settings

What Is LIV?

LIV is your AI-powered chat assistant that appears on your website. Visitors can ask questions and get instant responses about destinations, pricing, and travel planning.

Configuring LIV

System Instructions

Define how LIV should behave and respond:

  • Set the personality and tone (professional, friendly, casual, etc.)
  • Define expertise areas (Swedish travel, luxury experiences, etc.)
  • Specify response guidelines and limitations
  • Add company-specific information LIV should know

Knowledge Base

Upload documents that LIV can reference when answering questions:

  • Travel guides and itineraries
  • Pricing information
  • FAQs and common questions
  • Company policies

Greeting Messages

Customize the contextual greetings LIV shows based on which page visitors are on:

  • Homepage: General welcome message
  • Destinations: Destination-specific questions
  • Pricing: Pricing and booking questions
  • Contact: How can we help you today
Important: Test LIV thoroughly after making changes. Incorrect instructions can lead to poor user experiences.

Monitoring Conversations

Review conversation logs to:

  • Understand what visitors are asking about
  • Identify knowledge gaps to fill
  • Improve LIV's instructions
  • Find potential leads

Instagram Setup

What Is Instagram Integration?

The Instagram Setup page allows you to display your latest Instagram posts on your website's homepage, automatically pulling fresh content from your Instagram account.

Initial Setup

1Create a Facebook App (follow the detailed instructions on the Instagram Setup page)

2Set up Instagram Basic Display API

3Generate an access token

4Paste the access token in the CMS

5Click "Test Connection" to verify it works

6Save the token

Maintenance

Important: Instagram access tokens expire after 60 days. You'll need to regenerate and update the token every 2 months to keep the feed working.

What Displays on Your Site

  • Your 3 most recent Instagram posts
  • Post images
  • First line of each caption
  • Links back to Instagram
Tip: Set a calendar reminder to refresh your Instagram token every 50 days to avoid interruptions.

Blog Posts

What Are Blog Posts?

Blog posts are articles you publish on your website's blog/journal section. Use them to share travel tips, destination guides, company news, and stories.

Creating a Blog Post

1Click "Create New Post"

2Fill in the post details:

  • Title: Compelling headline (e.g., "10 Hidden Gems in Swedish Lapland")
  • Slug: URL-friendly version (auto-generated)
  • Featured Image: Eye-catching image shown in post listings
  • Excerpt: Short summary (150-250 characters)
  • Author: Select the post author from your team
  • Publish Date: When the post should be published
  • Categories: Select relevant categories (Travel Tips, Destinations, etc.)
  • Tags: Add keywords for better organization

3Write your content using the rich text editor:

  • Use headings (H2, H3) to structure content
  • Add images throughout the post
  • Include lists for better readability
  • Add links to relevant destinations or other posts

4Optimize for SEO:

  • Write a compelling meta description
  • Add alt text to all images
  • Use keywords naturally in your content
  • Include internal links to other pages

5Preview before publishing

6Set status to Published when ready

Best Practices

  • Aim for 800-1500 words for in-depth posts
  • Use high-quality images (at least 1200px wide)
  • Break up text with images, headings, and lists
  • Write engaging introductions to hook readers
  • Include a call-to-action at the end
  • Publish consistently (weekly or bi-weekly)
Tip: Schedule posts in advance by setting the publish date to a future date. The post will automatically go live at that time.

Stories

What Are Stories?

Stories are customer experiences, travel narratives, and testimonials that showcase the unique experiences your company provides. They're different from blog posts - they're more narrative and personal.

Creating a Story

1Click "Add New Story"

2Fill in story details:

  • Title: Story headline (e.g., "Sarah's Northern Lights Adventure")
  • Client Name: Name of the traveler (with permission)
  • Destination: Where the story takes place
  • Trip Date: When the journey occurred
  • Cover Image: Compelling photo from the trip
  • Story Type: Adventure, Romance, Family, Solo Travel, etc.

3Write the narrative:

  • Tell the story from the traveler's perspective
  • Include specific details and emotions
  • Highlight unique experiences
  • Add multiple images throughout

4Add a testimonial quote (optional but recommended)

5Save and publish

Story Best Practices

  • Get written permission from clients before publishing their stories
  • Use authentic, unedited photos when possible
  • Focus on emotional experiences, not just activities
  • Keep stories between 500-1000 words
  • Include specific details that paint a vivid picture
Note: Stories are powerful sales tools. They help potential clients envision their own experiences with your company.

Pillars

What Are Pillars?

Pillars are the core values, principles, or service pillars that define your company. They typically appear on your homepage and about page to communicate what makes your company unique.

Managing Pillars

1Click "Add New Pillar" (typically you'll have 3-6 pillars)

2Fill in pillar details:

  • Title: Pillar name (e.g., "Sustainable Travel", "Local Expertise", "Personalized Service")
  • Icon: Select an icon that represents this pillar
  • Description: Explain what this pillar means (2-3 sentences)
  • Order: Set the display order on your website

3Save and publish

Pillar Best Practices

  • Keep pillar titles short and punchy (2-3 words)
  • Write concise descriptions (50-100 words)
  • Make pillars actionable and specific to your business
  • Use positive, confident language
  • Ensure pillars differentiate you from competitors
Tip: Use the drag-and-drop feature to reorder pillars. Place your most important pillar first.

Pricing Tiers

What Are Pricing Tiers?

Pricing tiers are the different service levels or packages you offer, typically displayed on your pricing or services page.

Creating Pricing Tiers

1Click "Add Pricing Tier"

2Fill in tier details:

  • Tier Name: Package name (e.g., "Essential", "Luxury", "Ultimate")
  • Tagline: Brief description (e.g., "Perfect for first-time visitors")
  • Price: Starting price or price range
  • Price Note: Additional pricing context (e.g., "per person", "starting from")
  • Features: What's included in this tier (add multiple features)
  • Highlighted: Mark this tier as "Most Popular" or "Best Value"
  • Order: Display position (typically cheapest to most expensive)

3Add features:

  • List specific inclusions (accommodations, guides, meals, etc.)
  • Use checkmarks for included features
  • Optionally show what's NOT included with X marks

4Set CTA (Call-to-Action):

  • Button text (e.g., "Get Started", "Contact Us", "Book Now")
  • Button link (contact page, booking form, etc.)

5Save and publish

Pricing Strategy Tips

  • Offer 3-4 tiers maximum (too many causes decision paralysis)
  • Clearly differentiate what each tier includes
  • Highlight your recommended tier
  • Use anchoring (show highest price first to make others seem reasonable)
  • Be transparent about what's included vs. add-ons
Important: If you prefer not to show prices publicly, you can use text like "Custom Quote" or "Contact for Pricing" instead of specific amounts.

Media Library

What Is the Media Library?

The Media Library is your central repository for all images, documents, and files used across your website.

Uploading Files

1Click "Upload Files"

2Select files from your computer or drag-and-drop

3Wait for upload to complete

4Add file details:

  • Title: Descriptive name
  • Alt Text: Image description for accessibility and SEO
  • Caption: Optional text to display with image
  • Tags: Keywords for easy finding

5Save file information

Organizing Files

  • Use descriptive filenames before uploading
  • Tag files by category (destinations, team, blog, etc.)
  • Delete unused files periodically
  • Use search to find files quickly
  • Filter by file type (images, documents, videos)

Image Requirements

  • Hero Images: Minimum 1920x1080px, landscape orientation
  • Featured Images: Minimum 1200x800px
  • Team Photos: 500x500px, square format
  • Gallery Images: Minimum 1200px on longest side
  • File Format: JPG or PNG (WebP for smaller file sizes)
  • File Size: Optimize to under 500KB when possible

Using Media in Content

  • Click "Select from Media Library" when adding images to posts or pages
  • Search or filter to find the right image
  • Click image to insert it
  • Adjust size and alignment as needed
Important: Only upload images you have rights to use. Avoid using stock photos without proper licensing.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

I can't log in to the CMS

  • Check that you're using the correct email address
  • Ensure your password is correct (passwords are case-sensitive)
  • Try resetting your password using the "Forgot Password" link
  • Clear your browser cache and cookies
  • Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)

Changes aren't appearing on the website

  • Verify the content status is set to "Published" (not "Draft")
  • Hard refresh your browser (Ctrl+Shift+R or Cmd+Shift+R)
  • Clear your browser cache
  • Try viewing in an incognito/private browser window
  • Wait 1-2 minutes for changes to propagate

Images won't upload

  • Check file size - files over 10MB may fail to upload
  • Ensure file format is JPG, PNG, or WebP
  • Check your internet connection
  • Try compressing the image using a tool like TinyPNG
  • Rename file to remove special characters

The rich text editor isn't working

  • Refresh the page
  • Try a different browser
  • Disable browser extensions temporarily
  • Check for JavaScript errors in browser console (F12)

Instagram feed not updating

  • Check if your access token has expired (tokens last 60 days)
  • Regenerate a new access token following the setup instructions
  • Click "Test Connection" to verify the token works
  • Ensure your Instagram account is public

LIV AI giving incorrect responses

  • Review and update the system instructions
  • Check the knowledge base documents for accuracy
  • Remove outdated information from knowledge base
  • Test changes in the preview before publishing

Can't delete an item

  • Ensure you have permission to delete content
  • Check if the item is referenced elsewhere (e.g., blog post linked to destination)
  • Try refreshing the page and deleting again

  • Use modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge - latest versions)
  • Keep images optimized before uploading
  • Test changes in Draft mode before publishing
  • Back up important content before making major changes
  • Don't make changes from multiple devices simultaneously

For the best CMS experience, we recommend:

  • Great: Mozilla Firefox (latest version)

  • Good: Microsoft Edge (latest version)
